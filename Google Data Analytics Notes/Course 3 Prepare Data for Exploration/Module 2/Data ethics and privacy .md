# Essential data ethics

### Key Points

- **Data Ethics:** Well-founded standards of right and wrong guiding how data is collected, shared, and used.
- **Importance:**
    - Relatively new field with evolving rules and regulations.
    - Addresses concerns about data privacy and responsible use.
    - Ensures fairness and minimizes bias in data analysis.
- **Six Key Aspects:**
    1. **Ownership:** Individuals own their data and control its usage, processing, and sharing.
    2. **Transaction Transparency:** Data processing and algorithms should be transparent and understandable to individuals.
    3. **Consent:** Individuals have the right to know how and why their data will be used before providing it.
    4. **Currency:** Individuals should be aware of financial transactions resulting from their data usage.
    5. **Privacy:** Protecting individuals' data from unauthorized access and use.
    6. **Openness:** Making data accessible and understandable to the public.

### Definitions

- **Data Bias:** Systematic errors skewing results in a specific direction, potentially leading to negative consequences.
- **Marginalized Groups:** Groups often misrepresented by biased data due to their underrepresentation in data sets.

# Prioritize data privacy

## **What is Data Privacy?**

- Data privacy refers to the protection of an individual's information and activity whenever data is involved.
- It encompasses access, use, and collection of data, along with an individual's legal rights over their data.
- This includes protection from unauthorized access, freedom from inappropriate use, the right to inspect, update, or correct data, the ability to consent to data usage, and the legal right to access one's own data.

## **Why is Data Privacy Important?**

- Data privacy is crucial for individuals and companies alike.
- For individuals, it ensures control over their personal information and protects them from misuse.
- For companies, it builds trust with customers and ensures compliance with data protection regulations.

## **Data Privacy Legislation**

- Recognizing the importance of data privacy, governments worldwide have implemented data protection legislation.
- These laws aim to safeguard individuals and their data, providing them with legal recourse in case of privacy violations.

## **The Importance of Trust**

- Trust is a critical element in data privacy.
- Individuals need to trust companies with their data, which encourages them to use products and services and share information.
- Companies have a responsibility to earn and maintain this trust by implementing robust data privacy measures.

# Data anonymization

## What is data anonymization?

- You have been learning about the importance of privacy in data analytics.
- Now, it is time to talk about **data anonymization** and what types of data should be anonymized. **Personally identifiable information**, or **PII**, is information that can be used by itself or with other data to track down a person's identity.
- Data anonymization is the process of protecting people's private or sensitive data by eliminating that kind of information.
- Typically, data anonymization involves blanking, hashing, or masking personal information, often by using fixed-length codes to represent data columns, or hiding data with altered values.

### **Your role in data anonymization**

- Organizations have a responsibility to protect their data and the personal information that data might contain. A
- s a data analyst, you might be expected to understand what data needs to be anonymized, but you generally wouldn't be responsible for the data anonymization itself.
- A rare exception might be if you work with a copy of the data for testing or development purposes. In this case, you could be required to anonymize the data before you work with it.

## What types of data should be anonymized?

- Healthcare and financial data are two of the most sensitive types of data.
- These industries rely a lot on data anonymization techniques.
- After all, the stakes are very high. That’s why data in these two industries usually goes through **de-identification**, which is **a process used to wipe data clean of all personally identifying information**.
- Data anonymization is used in just about every industry. That is why it is so important for data analysts to understand the basics. Here is a list of data that is often anonymized:
    - Telephone numbers
    - Names
    - License plates and license numbers
    - Social security numbers
    - IP addresses
    - Medical records
    - Email addresses
    - Photographs
    - Account numbers
- For some people, it just makes sense that this type of data should be anonymized. For others, we have to be very specific about what needs to be anonymized.
- Imagine a world where we all had access to each other’s addresses, account numbers, and other identifiable information. That would invade a lot of people’s privacy and make the world less safe. Data anonymization is one of the ways we can keep data private and secure!

# The ethical use of data

### **Key Points**

- **Unfair Biases:** Algorithms and data sets can amplify or reinforce unfair biases, potentially harming underrepresented communities and minority groups.
- **Decision-Making Processes:** Algorithms and data sets are used in various decision-making processes, such as content curation and loan eligibility.
- **Responsible Use of Data and AI:** The field, industry, and community are learning about the responsible use of data and AI.
- **Democratization of Responsible AI:** It's crucial to democratize the responsible use of AI to ensure ethical development and implementation.
- **Benefits of AI and Data:** AI and data can improve lives, enable new possibilities, and provide opportunities for innovation.
- **Collective Responsibility:** Everyone, including non-technologists, needs to be involved in the ethical development and use of AI.

### **Definitions**

- **Ethical AI:** The development and use of AI in a way that is fair, unbiased, and beneficial to society.
- **Data Bias:** The presence of systematic errors or distortions in data that can lead to unfair or discriminatory outcomes.
- **Algorithmic Bias:** The tendency of algorithms to perpetuate or amplify existing biases in data.
- **Democratization of AI:** Making AI accessible and understandable to everyone, not just experts.
